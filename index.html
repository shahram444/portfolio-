How Dimensions Shape Transport: When 2D Isn't Enough

I work on a fundamental problem in geosciences: Can two-dimensional pore-scale models truly represent three-dimensional reality?

Using lattice Boltzmann simulations (CompLaB), I compare permeability, breakthrough curves, fluid age, spreading & mixing behavior, and connectivity-driven heterogeneity across synthetic porous media.

Anaerobic Oxidation of Methane (AOM) in Pore Networks

I build pore-scale models of AOM at the sulfate‚Äìmethane transition zone to understand how methane and sulfate meet in tortuous pores, why microbial hotspots emerge, and how geometry limits or enhances reaction zones.

This work ties marine geochemistry to microbial ecology ‚Äî it's about understanding the invisible and the essential.

Representing Microbes in Complex Pore Structures

In an ongoing review paper, I'm synthesizing how microbial processes are modeled at the pore scale‚Äîeverything from pore geometry extraction to reaction coupling and biofilm representation.

This project asks: How do we make pore-scale models both realistic and computationally meaningful?

About Me

I grew up in Iran, surrounded by big landscapes and small mysteries ‚Äî mountains, dust, rivers, and the quiet geometry of rocks. I was always drawn to the spaces beneath what we see: the subsurface, the microscopic, the hidden.

My path to pore-scale modeling wasn't direct. It grew from a curiosity about how the smallest structures‚Äîpore throats, grains, microbes‚Äîgovern large-scale processes like methane cycling and solute transport.

When I moved to the U.S. for my Ph.D., I stepped into a world where physics, geology, computation, and chemistry meet in nonlinear ways. I learned to generate porous media from algorithms, to simulate fluids through tiny constrictions, and to translate complex transport behavior into equations and maps.

Athens, Georgia became home. Long simulation runs became part of my days, and my two cats, Jamshid and Sevda, became my nightly coworkers.

Outside research, I care deeply about literature, Persian poetry, film, astronomy, and the emotional & philosophical undercurrents of life.

My scientific approach is interdisciplinary, patient, and detail-driven ‚Äî shaped by a belief that understanding the smallest scales changes how we see the world as a whole.
